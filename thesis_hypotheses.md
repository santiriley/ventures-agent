## Hypothesis 1: Vertical AI Ownership

The shift: founders moving from "build tools for an industry" to "become the
industry, powered by AI." Not selling software to law firms; becoming the law
firm. Not supporting logistics operators; being the freight forwarder with AI
doing 80% of the work and a thin human layer for judgment.

CA/DR angle: professional services in the region are fragmented, expensive
relative to local purchasing power, and underserved outside capital cities.
Regulatory fragmentation across 7+ countries is a moat, not just a problem,
because a multi-country AI compliance or service engine is hard to replicate.
A founder with domain experience can serve the entire region from one country.

Portfolio echo: Snap Compliance (AI-native regtech), Socialdesk (AI customer
service). Pattern: AI replaces the service layer, software is delivery.

Look for: founders with 5+ years domain experience in a CA/DR professional
service (legal, accounting, customs, insurance, HR) who are building an
AI-native version of that service, not a tool for incumbents.

## Hypothesis 2: Enterprise AI Infrastructure (Picks and Shovels)

The shift: enterprises adopting AI need infrastructure — data pipelines,
fine-tuning, deployment, monitoring, compliance tooling, governance. The
companies building this layer for enterprise are a proven global category.

CA/DR angle: large regional employers (banks, telcos, free trade zone
manufacturers, agricultural exporters, retail chains) are starting AI adoption.
They need tooling that works with Spanish-language data, local compliance
frameworks, and regional integration patterns. A CA/DR founder has a natural
wedge with regional enterprises before competing globally.

Portfolio echo: Snap Compliance (GRC for regulated entities), Avify (commerce
infra for SMEs). Both sell infrastructure to businesses. The new version is
AI-specific infrastructure.

Look for: B2B tools helping LATAM enterprises deploy, monitor, or govern AI.
Also: AI-native versions of existing enterprise workflows (procurement, HR,
supply chain) built for the regional context.

## Hypothesis 3: Wealth Management Disruption

The shift: wealth management is unbundling beyond robo-advisors. AI-powered
financial planning, automated tax optimization, democratized access to
alternative assets, and embedded wealth features inside neobanks and payroll.

CA/DR angle: wealth management in the region is dominated by traditional banks
and family offices. Almost no digital-native option exists for the emerging
middle class or high-earning professionals. The diaspora population (US-based
CA/DR professionals with savings) is also underserved.

Portfolio echo: O$MO (investment/trading access), Tumoni (neobank for migrants),
Ábaco (SME credit). The fund understands financial services distribution here.

Look for: fintech founders going beyond payments into wealth building, savings
automation, or alternative asset access (real estate fractionalization, private
credit) for CA/DR consumers or diaspora.

## Hypothesis 4: Climate and Sustainability Tech

The shift: carbon markets, circular economy, waste-to-value, and climate
adaptation are scaling. Regulatory pressure (EU CBAM, SEC climate disclosure)
creates compliance-driven demand globally.

CA/DR angle: the region has natural advantages in carbon offsets, biodiversity
credits, and sustainable agriculture. Costa Rica's green brand is an export
product. Opportunity for founders to build measurement, verification, and
trading infrastructure for regional climate assets, or climate-adapted versions
of traditional industries.

Portfolio echo: Vitrinnea (circular economy), Kleantab (eco-friendly CPG).

Look for: climate measurement/verification tools, carbon/biodiversity credit
platforms, climate-adapted supply chain solutions, sustainable CPG with tech
distribution, anything leveraging CA/DR's natural advantages.

## Hypothesis 5: Mobility and Transport Tech

The shift: urban mobility, logistics routing, fleet management, and
micro-mobility are being rebuilt with software. Electric vehicle infrastructure
is early but accelerating in emerging markets.

CA/DR angle: transport infrastructure in the region has real gaps. Public
transit is fragmented, last-mile delivery is expensive, cross-border freight
is manual and paper-heavy. A tech layer on top of regional transport is
underbuilt compared to Mexico, Brazil, or Colombia.

Portfolio echo: Boxful (last-mile logistics, multi-carrier aggregation).

Look for: founders building fleet management, route optimization, cross-border
freight digitization, micro-mobility, or EV infrastructure for CA/DR markets.
Also: transport-adjacent plays like insurance or financing for vehicle fleets.
